Love Your Life After Divorce!

Until you do a complete marriage autopsy, your relationship patterns will repeat in their typically predictable way. Why not try and understand what actually happened so you can stop making the same mistakes?

" Divorce is the beginning of a new chapter. A time in your life which you get to be the person you always dreamed you could be! "

No one walks down the aisle on their wedding day thinking their relationship will end in a divorce. Unfortunately, according to the American Psychological Association, more than 90 percent of people marry by age 50. However, 40 to 50 percent of married couples in the United States divorce. The divorce rate for subsequent marriages is even higher.

Numbers don’t lie and seldom do couples do their homework before entering relationships or picking new life partners. Let me introduce you to The Marriage Autopsy.

This idea came from my own divorce. It’s a valuable tool to help you sort out the things in your marriage that worked, didn’t work, and what you’d like to do differently next time. A Marriage Autopsy creates clarity and self-confidence. It can help you move forward with a healthy outlook. It can bridge the gap between where you are and where you’d like to be. The best time to do an autopsy is when one relationship ends and before a new one begins.

Until you do a complete marriage autopsy, your relationship patterns will repeat in their typically predictable way. Why not try and understand what actually happened so you can stop making the same mistakes?

While many couples see remarriage as a second chance at happiness, the statistics tell a different story. The divorce rate for second marriages in the US is over 60%!
