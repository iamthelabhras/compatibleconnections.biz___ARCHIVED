One-on-One Coaching

A great coach can change a life!

They see what you can be rather than what you already are.

Hey, Thinking About Coaching With Me?

You should be if you’re feeling lost, overwhelmed, or perplexed about dating again after divorce!

Maybe you're already dating but you're struggling to have fun and meet great guys. Or maybe you're not ready to date just yet but would like some pointers on how to overcome your fears and start putting yourself out there.

Whatever it is, dating after divorce takes courage, confidence, and a willingness to learn a whole new set of dating skills!

Realizing that you need to do something to move yourself and your life forward is the first and most difficult step to take. By logging onto this site, you've already taken that first step. Well done!

Let me ask you, though: have you worked with a dating coach before?

If you haven't there are some simple - but important - things you need to understand before we get started:

One-on-one coaching isn’t like counseling.

We won’t be rehashing the past or looking at what went wrong in your marriage. We'll be starting from where you are right now and moving forward from there.

One-on-one coaching is flexible & client-centered.

You can work with me in several different ways: whatever suits you, your time, your preferred learning style, and your budget.

One-on-one coaching requires your active participation & commitment.

It's both a financial and personal investment in yourself and your future.

The women I work with come away from coaching feeling inspired, refreshed, encouraged, and empowered ... but only if they're open to learning new skills, rethinking old ideas about love and romance, and are ready to invest their time & energy in personal growth.

Ask yourself:

  • Are you open, willing, and able to make changes?
  • Are you highly motivated to do things differently?
  • ️Are you willing to learn new approaches?
  • Is your belief system flexible enough to try on new ideas?
  • Are you excited about moving yourself and your life forward?

If you answered “yes” to these questions, then you’re a perfect candidate for coaching!

What Is Coaching?

Coaching is not counseling.

Coaches do not "diagnose" clients' problems or tell people what they need to do to "get better". Coaches believe that nobody understands you better than yourself and that, in a very real way, you already "know" the answers to your questions and the solutions to your problems.

Coaches believe that their role in the coach-client relationship is to help clients uncover these answers and solutions. To facilitate this process of discovery, coaches are trained to ask empowering, transformational questions that help clients create solutions, generate new ideas and insights, and overcome their life's greatest challenges. They're also trained to be great listeners whose focus is always on what makes each individual client unique.

Coaching is about helping people unlock their own potential and realize their life's possibilities!

Is There A Professional Designation For Coaches?

Yes there is!

It's called the Master Certified Coach ("MCC") credential and it's awarded by the International Coach Federation ("ICF").

Before aspiring coaches can become MCCs, they must complete 200 hours of coursework and extensive training, log 2500 hours of one-on-one coaching work with clients and pass a comprehensive & rigorous examination.

This certification process usually takes about two years to complete.

Coach Bernadette earned her Master Certified Coach credential in 2003 and, since then, has acquired over a decade's worth of experience coaching single and divorced women.
